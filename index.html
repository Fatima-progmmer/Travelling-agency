Johannesburg

Emirates Gateway

Johannesburg, the "e Goli", is a sprawling metropolis, where more than a mile below the bustling city traffic, miners dig for the world's most precious metal, if you stand on the top floor of the Carlton Centre, the city's highest building, you will see gold mine dumps and shaft head gears dotting the skyline. For a more leisurely way of observing the gold mining industry, visit Gold Reef City where you can watch the complete mining process.

Shopping is a delight at Rosebank, Sandton, Fourways and at Eastgate, and the many flea markets that sprout up all over town on the weekends, offer ideal opportunities to purchase South African arts, crafts and curios.

The choice of international restaurants is extensive and the food and wine excellent and very good value for money, Johannesburg is well-known for is nightlife, however, the trendiest spots are Posetsarik, Sandton, Ilovo, Rivonia, Fourways and Pretoria. Cape Town is only two hours away by air.

Health requirements:

No compulsory vaccinations are required. However, a yellow fever certificate is necessary if arriving from infected areas. Typhoid, hepatitis A, polio and tetanus immunisations are recommended and

in some circumstances, diphtheria and hepatitis B are also advisable. Precautions should be taken against malaria. Please seek further advice from your doctor.

Note: Malaria (throughout the year in the low altitude areas of Mpumalanga and north-eastern Kwa-Zulu Natal as far south as the Tugela river) and rabies are present. The bilharzia parasite is present in stagnant water in some rural areas.

Lightweight clothing is advisable in the summer, with warm clothing for the winter. An umbrella is required during the summer in Johannesburg and also during the winter months in the Cape Province.

Climate:

South Africa's climate varies from temperate to subtropical. Johannesburg, in the Gauteng region, experiences hot summers with intermittent showers and cold, frosty winters. Cape Town in the Western Cape Province has a Mediterranean climate, with maximum rainfall during winter. The rest of the country, including the Northern and Eastern Cape, Mpumalanga and north-eastem Kwa-Zulu Natal has rainfall during the summer months with the eastern and southern coastal regions experiencing more than average rainfall.

Value Added Tax (VAT) Refund Scheme:

Currently set at 14 per cent, VAT is included in the marked or quoted price of most goods and services. Identify yourself as a tourist and request an original tax invoice or a VAT 263 certificate when you purchase goods.

Getting around:

It is difficult for visitors to catch buses and trains as public transport networks are set up mainly for the local commuter. In cities, tourists are therefore advised to travel by taxi, even though they are more expensive. Clients should always ask the hotel to book a tad for them as hotels have reliable and safe ted companies that they use regularly.

Driving licence:

Driving is on the left-hand side of the road and an International Driving Permit is required along with your local driving license from your country of residence. Traffic laws are strictly enforced, so it is worth familiarising yourself with them.
